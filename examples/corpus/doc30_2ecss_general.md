# 2-edge-connected spanning subgraph in general graphs
The minimum-size 2-edge-connected spanning subgraph problem is APX-hard in general graphs.
We survey approximation ratios from 5/4 (subcubic) to roughly 4/3 (general) and the underlying techniques.
LP-based reasoning, ear decompositions, and matching subroutines all appear in various analyses.
We compare combinatorial and rounding-based approaches and outline open ratio improvements.
