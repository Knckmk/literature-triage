# Jain's 2-approximation for survivable network design
Jain's algorithm gives a 2-approximation for survivable network design via iterative LP rounding.
The key insight is a half-integrality property: any basic feasible solution has at least one variable of value at least 1/2.
We summarize the proof of half-integrality and its dependence on uncrossing arguments.
Open problems include matching this ratio with combinatorial techniques.
